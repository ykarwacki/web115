Frequently Asked Questions

How old do I have to be?

You must be at least 18 years old and have a valid state ID. Your parent or guardian can give consent for us to pierce if you are under age. Anyone under 18 in the shop must be accompanied by a parent or guardian and have the IDs to prove it.

Is it safe?

We follow a very strict sanitary procedure. All equipment and ink is fully sterilized and used only once. Because of this there is truly minimal risk of infection of any kind.

Can I bring my own jewelry?

NO. The jewelry we use for your initial piercing is part of our sanitary practices. We use only surgical grade titanium, because we have found it to be least likely to cause an allergic reaction. The jewelry you select is new and fully sterilized.

What kind of jewelry do you have?

Our jewelry is made of implant quality surgical titanium, and there are many different styles to choose from along with dangling charms and different colored semi-precious stones.

Do piercings hurt?

Generally, not much or not at all. Even piercings of extra sensitive areas are over on fractions of a second and a properly preformed piercing doesn't create any residule pain. There is sometimes some discomfort during the healing process as your body adjusts to the new piercing.

How should I care for my piercing?

Always throughly wash your hands before touching your piercing or jewelry and change clothing in contact with the area often. Wash the area once or twice daily with fragrance-free anti-bacterial soap and rinse throughly. DO NOT apply alcohol, hydrogen perioxide, betadine, neosporin, or any other disinfectant. These will interfere with your skins natural defenses. During the first few weeks, you should soak your piercing in warm salt water for about five minutes 2 or 3 times a day. You should not change jewelry until healing is complete.

Healing time will vary greatly with the person and the type of piercing. Any healing time suggested for your piercing is a rough estimate. If you feel your having an usually difficult time healing, you can come by the shop - sometimes we can help. But please remember, we are not doctors. If you see signs of infections - swelling, discharge, heat, pain - its safest to see a doctor.
