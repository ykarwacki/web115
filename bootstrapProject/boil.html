The Boil Developing flavors

The main purpose of the boil is to disolve hop oils in the water, but unnecessary proteins will also coagulate so they can be easily removed and not contribute to haze.

The boil will go very much like it does when brewing with malt extract. If your lauter ends with signifcantly more wort than 6 gallons, you have two options: brew a larger batch or boil the wort down to 6 gallons before adding the boiling hops (this will give you a 5 gallon batch with higher original gravity). At the end of the boil, use your wort chiller to cool the wort to below 80 degrees F as quickily as possible. Transfer the wort to your primary fermentation vessel, aerate the wort, pitch the yeast, and store the fermenting beer in an area with a stable temperature in the appropriate range for the yeast strain.
